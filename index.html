Terje "Valfar" Bakken

A gifted multi-instrumentalist whose life was cut short.

Terje "Valfar" Bakken was born on September 3rd, 1978 in the town of Sogndal in Sogn og Fjordane county, Norway and is best known for his famous musical project "Windir", founded in 1994. Originally trained on an accordion, he would go on to become a talented guitarist, bassist, drummer, synthesizer, keyboardist, vocalist and programmer. Up until 2001 all of Windir's albums were recorded solely by Valfar with a multitrack recorder.

Valfar drew heavy inspiration from traditional folk music and Scandanavian folklore and mythology, even performing in the archaic local dialect Sognamål. His songs often mixed black metal with folk music and electronica; somewhat genre defying at the time, Valfar described Windir as "Sognametal".

On January 17th, 2004, Terje "Valfar" Bakken was found dead in the Sogndal Valley. He was caught in a snow storm while hiking towards his family's cabin and died of hypothermia. He was buried at Stedje Church in Norway on January 27th.
